# Block D: Stakeholders and Interaction Priorities

## Purpose
Systematization of potential partners, licensees, competitors, and other market (ecosystem) participants relevant for cooperation, licensing, or negotiations. The main task is to determine who to interact with, in what order, and why.

## Stakeholder
Designation: I
Any market (ecosystem) participant capable of influencing architectural development or benefiting from its application.

## Interaction Priority
Designation: Pr
Pr = 3 — high
Pr = 2 — medium
Pr = 1 — low

## Architectural Intersection
Designation: Kₐ
The degree of intersection of the stakeholder's products or technologies with your own architecture and patents.

## Potential Pain
Designation: Bₚ
The set of the stakeholder's problems that are resolved by the proposed architectural solution.

## Deal Potential
Designation: Vₚ
Assessment of the possible economic value of cooperation.

## Interaction Strategy
Letter, phone call, NDA, face-to-face meeting, demonstration, licensing, joint development.

## Interaction Status
Not contacted; letter sent; response received; negotiations; agreement; refusal; follow-up.

## Main Goal
To create an interaction architecture where cooperation becomes the most profitable scenario for all participants.

## Main Question of the Architect
Who needs to be interacted with first so that the architectural change brings maximum added value with minimum time and resource expenditure?
