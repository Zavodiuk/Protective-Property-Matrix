# Block C: Market (Ecosystem) and Industry Architecture

## Purpose
Research of the market (ecosystem), search for architectural deserts, analysis of players, industry maturity, and directions where architectural change can create maximum added value.

## Market (Ecosystem)
Designation: M
The sum of all elements influencing architectural development: companies, consumers, manufacturers, suppliers, research, technologies, standards, legislation, patents, investments, logistics, information environment, competitors.

## Architectural Desert
Designation: Dₐ
An area of the market (ecosystem) with a minimal number of strong architectural solutions and patents.

## Market Maturity
Designation: Z
0 ≤ Z ≤ 1
0 — market not ready.
1 — market fully ready.

## Architectural Density
Designation: ρₐ
The number of architectural solutions or patents per unit of the studied area.

## Architectural Intersection
Designation: Kₐ
The degree of intersection of the new architecture with existing architectural solutions.

## Competitor
A competitor is viewed as a carrier of a specific architecture, not just as a company.

## Market (Ecosystem) Research
- Building a global market (ecosystem) map.
- Clustering companies by regions and segments.
- Searching for architectural deserts.
- Determining market maturity.
- Analyzing competitors' architecture.
- Analyzing global technology trends.
- Analyzing standards, regulation, and patent environment.
- Identifying the most promising directions for development.

## Main Goal
To find directions where architectural change can create maximum added value.

## Main Question of the Architect
Where will an architectural change produce the maximum effect with minimum market (ecosystem) resistance?
