# Block A: Architecture of Decision Making

## Purpose
Conversion of the philosophy of architectural thinking into the decision-making process. If Block G answers the question "How does the architect think?", Block A answers the question "How does the architect make decisions?".

## The 8 Laws of Decision Making
1. Architecture is above details.
2. Intent is above technology.
3. The system is optimized, not the object. Example: Human + Glove + Work + Environment.
4. Pain determines value.
5. Market readiness is mandatory.
6. A patent is a tool for managing market architecture.
7. The architect changes the system, not improves a separate detail.
8. Architecture is born from the synthesis of multiple weak signals.

## Sequence of Decision Making
1. Researching the environment
2. Determining market maturity
3. Searching for pain
4. Forming a goal
5. Forming an intent
6. Building architecture
7. Searching for technical implementation
8. Checking architecture
9. Patenting
10. Scaling

## Main Goal
To create such an architectural solution where the benefit of cooperation significantly exceeds the cost of conflict.

## Main Question of the Architect
What needs to be changed in the system's architecture to obtain maximum added value?
