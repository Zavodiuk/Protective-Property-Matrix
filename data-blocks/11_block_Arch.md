# Block Arch: The Architect

## Purpose
To define the architect's role in the development of any system. The architect is the source of architectural change. They do not serve the existing system — they create a new one.

## Who is the Architect
The architect is a subject capable of observing reality, seeing the system as a whole, discovering contradictions, forming a concept, creating a new architecture, and taking responsibility for the consequences of its change.

## Main Principle
The architect does not receive the right to create a new architecture. They create it because the existing architecture has ceased to correspond to the changed reality.

## Second Principle
The architect does not change details. The architect changes the connections between details. It is the change in connections that changes the behavior of the entire system.

## Third Principle
The architect does not wait for permission. They offer the system a more viable architecture. If the new architecture is objectively better, reality gradually begins to rebuild around it.

## The Architect's Main Resource
Not money. Not power. Not technology. The architect's main resource is the ability to see what others do not yet see.

## Main Responsibility
Any architectural change alters the future of the system. Therefore, the architect is responsible not only for the architecture created but also for the consequences of its appearance.

## Main Task
Do not improve the past. Create the future.

## Main Criterion
The architect is evaluated not by the number of objects created, but by the magnitude of the change to the system's architecture.

## Main Goal
To create an architecture whose viability is higher than the existing one.

## Main Question
What new architecture is capable of making the system more viable than the existing one?

## The Architect's Main Law
If the existing architecture has ceased to correspond to reality, the architect is obligated to seek a new architecture.
