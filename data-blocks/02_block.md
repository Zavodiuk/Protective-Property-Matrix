# Block G: Philosophy and Laws of Architectural Thinking
## Purpose
The formation of the architect's worldview, defining observation, thinking, and decision-making.
## The 11 Laws of Architecture
1. Architecture is above details.
2. Intent is above technology.
3. The system is more important than the object.
4. Pain births value.
5. Understanding is born from the synthesis of multiple weak signals.
6. Trust cannot be bought; it can only be built.
7. Any complex system evolves through architecture, not random improvements.
8. Patents are tools for managing market architecture.
9. The architect seeks not answers, but the right questions.
10. Real value arises when an architectural change benefits all participants.
11. Any architecture exists solely to achieve the goal of the initiator.
## Core Concept
Architectural thinking is the ability to see the system as a whole, understand its laws, find weak spots, and change the direction of evolution.
## Long-term Goal
To create a universal architectural approach for tech, materials, AI, business, and complex systems.
